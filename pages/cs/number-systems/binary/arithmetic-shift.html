Binary Shift Operations for Arithmetic

Binary shift operations are fundamental low-level operations that efficiently perform multiplication or division by powers of two. These operations are integral to digital circuit design, compilers, and performance-critical code.

Left Shift ($\ll$): Multiplication by Powers of 2

A binary left shift shifts all bits of a number to the left by a given number of positions, inserting zeros on the right. Mathematically, shifting a binary number $n$ to the left by $k$ positions is equivalent to multiplying it by $2^k$:

$$ n \ll k = n \times 2^k $$

Example: Shift $n = 001101$ left by $2$ positions

Original:   001101   (decimal 13)
Shifted:   110100   (decimal 52)
Math:      13 × 2^2 = 52

Each left shift moves bits to higher powers of two, increasing the magnitude of the value.

Right Shift ($\gg$): Integer Division by Powers of 2

A binary right shift moves all bits to the right, discarding the least significant bits and (for unsigned values) inserting zeros on the left. This operation approximates integer division by a power of two:

$$ n \gg k = \left\lfloor \frac{n}{2^k} \right\rfloor $$

Example: Shift $n = 110100$ right by $2$ positions

Original:   110100   (decimal 52)
Shifted:    001101   (decimal 13)
Math:       52 ÷ 2^2 = 13

Each right shift reduces the magnitude, similar to integer division. The floor operation ensures any fractional bits are discarded.

Arithmetic vs Logical Shifts

There are two kinds of right shifts: logical and arithmetic. For unsigned values, both behave identically. For signed values, the distinction becomes important: 

Logical Right Shift:
Fills left bits with 0 regardless of sign

Arithmetic Right Shift:
Preserves sign bit (for two's complement negative numbers)

Example: Arithmetic right shift of $n = 11110000$ (−16 in two's complement) by 1: 

Original:   11110000   (−16)
Shifted:    11111000   (−8)

The sign is preserved due to replication of the MSB (most significant bit), which represents the sign in two's complement.

Summary of Shift Operations

Left Shift (n << k):     n × 2^k     (Multiplication)
Right Shift (n >> k):    ⌊n / 2^k⌋   (Division, floor)
Shift type depends on:   Sign and system (logical vs arithmetic)
Used for:                Fast math, loop optimization, hardware logic
